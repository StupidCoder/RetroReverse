Marble Madness (Amiga) — course viewer

tilemap: drag to pan · slopes: drag to rotate · scroll to zoom

Each course is the actual playfield tilemap, decoded from its .mlb map file and assembled tile-by-tile exactly as the game's blitter does — a 36-tile-wide column of 8×8, 16-colour tiles, in the course's own palette (the isometric look is painted into the tile art itself, not a 3-D projection). Drag to pan, scroll to zoom; the marble starts at the top and the GOAL is at the bottom. Tick Slopes (3D) to switch to the course's height field — the surface the marble physically rolls on, reconstructed from the Track file's slope regions (the engine's own build_region mesh) and drawn as an isometric wireframe with hidden lines removed — the same style as the write-up's slope renders. Edges are coloured low→high and the height is slightly exaggerated to read the gentle ramps; drag to rotate (the pivot follows the point you're looking at), scroll to zoom. Objects then overlays the Track-layer markers — object placements (cyan), the dynamic/animated regions (yellow), and the creature patrol routes (black marbles magenta, slinkies green) — the same overlays as the write-up's slope renders. See the write-up for how the tile format was decoded.
